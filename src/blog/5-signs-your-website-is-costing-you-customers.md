A website isn't a brochure you build once and forget. For a local business, it's the difference between a phone that rings and a phone that doesn't. Here are five signs yours is working against you.

## 1. It takes more than 3 seconds to load

More than half of visitors leave a page that takes longer than three seconds. If your site is slow, you're paying for traffic that bounces before it ever sees what you offer.

## 2. It looks broken on a phone

The majority of local searches happen on mobile. If customers have to pinch, zoom, and squint, they'll back out and call the next business on the list.

## 3. There's no clear way to contact you

Every page should make the next step obvious: **a tap-to-call button and a short form, above the fold.** If a customer has to hunt for your phone number, you've already lost some of them.

## 4. It doesn't show up on Google

A beautiful site nobody can find is a billboard in the desert. If you're not ranking for the services you offer in the towns you serve, your site needs local SEO — not just a fresh coat of paint.

## 5. It hasn't changed since 2018

Design trends, browsers, and Google's expectations move fast. An outdated site quietly signals "this business might be outdated too." First impressions are everything.
