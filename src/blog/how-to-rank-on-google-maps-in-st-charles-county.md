When someone in St. Peters, O'Fallon, or St. Charles pulls out their phone and searches "plumber near me" or "best tacos near me," Google shows them a map with **three businesses** at the top. That's the Map Pack — and those three spots get the lion's share of the calls.

If you're not one of them, you're invisible to the people who are ready to buy *right now*.

Here's how to fix that.

## 1. Claim and complete your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile is the single biggest factor in local rankings. Most businesses set it up once and forget it. Don't.

- Fill out **every** field: hours, services, service areas, website, phone.
- Pick the most specific primary category you can (e.g., "Emergency Plumber," not just "Plumber").
- Add real photos — businesses with photos get **more clicks and direction requests**.

## 2. Get consistent across the web

Google trusts businesses whose **Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP)** match everywhere they appear — your site, Yelp, Facebook, directories. Even small differences ("St." vs "Saint," an old phone number) chip away at your ranking.

> Consistency is boring. It's also one of the highest-ROI things a local business can do.

## 3. Earn reviews — and respond to them

Reviews are rocket fuel for local rankings, and they convince customers to choose you.

- Ask every happy customer for a review (a quick text with a link works best).
- Respond to **all** of them — good and bad. Google notices, and so do customers.

## 4. Make your website fast and local

Google reads your website to understand who you serve. Mention the towns you work in, load in under two seconds, and make sure it works perfectly on a phone.
